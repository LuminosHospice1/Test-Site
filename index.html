FireNote System — Discharge Workflow — Step-by-step walkthrough for processing any discharge type in FireNote, including care plan rules, patient status updates, and HOPE assessment responsibilities. Use the tabs above for discharge-type-specific documentation checklists.
📋 Core Discharge Steps in FireNote
1
Start a Discharge Encounter 👩‍⚕️ Discharging Nurse
Navigate to Patient > Encounters > Discharge
Complete all applicable assessments within the encounter, then add a Discharge Order in the Care Plan under the primary diagnosis. This must be done inside the discharge encounter.
2
Care Plan — Discontinue Meds / DME / Supplies / Orders 👩‍⚕️ Discharging Nurse
Make all changes from within the Care Plan. Changes here flow automatically into the Discharge Summary. 🚫 Live discharges & transfers — do NOT resolve the care plan. Resolving removes medications from the summary. The patient's next provider needs that full medication list. ✅ Death — RN attending: Resolve all care plan problems except the primary diagnosis. The primary diagnosis is resolved at the IDG meeting. Count and document each narcotic before discontinuing. ℹ️ Death — LPN attending: LPNs cannot resolve care plan problems. Leave all items in place — IDG will resolve everything at the IDG meeting.
3
Update Patient Status 🏥 Director of Clinical Services
Navigate to Patient Info > Care Periods
Update: Status, Date/Time, Discharge Reason, then click Update. Level of Care changes are always completed by the Director of Clinical Services.
4
IDG Review / Sign-Off 🤝 RN Case Manager & IDG Team
If primary goals are met, create a custom goal (example: "Discharge Planning") for IDG sign-off. All IDG disciplines must be documented as involved in the discharge plan.
⏱️ Timing Rules — One Encounter Per Care Period

There is only one discharge encounter per care period. Timing depends on the discharge type:

Live Discharge
The discharge encounter must be started before the discharge date/time. Applies to: Revocation, No Longer Terminally Ill, Moved Out of Service Area, Transfer to Another Hospice, and Discharge for Cause.
Death Discharge
The discharge encounter must be started at or after the time of death — never before the documented time of death.
📊 HOPE Record — Responsibilities
👩‍⚕️ Discharging Nurse
Responsible for completing the Discharge HOPE assessment in FireNote. The HOPE record is automatically created when patient status is set to Discharged or Expired. Complete the assessment before closing out the discharge.
🏢 Back Office
Responsible for ensuring the HOPE discharge record is submitted to iQIES on time. The nurse does not need to manage the submission deadline — hand off to back office once the assessment is complete.

Revocation (Section A) — The patient or DPOA voluntarily chooses to end hospice benefits. This is a beneficiary-driven discharge. No physician order is required. Your documentation should capture a picture of the full hospice stay and the patient's current presentation — think of it as a handoff note to whoever takes over their care.
🖥️ FireNote: Start the encounter before the discharge date/time. Do not resolve the care plan. Status update (Level of Care change) is completed by the Director of Clinical Services in Patient Info > Care Periods. Complete your HOPE assessment — back office handles submission.

Section B — Death Checklist

0 / 8▼
i. Discharge encounter completed — started at or after time of death Patient > Encounters > Discharge. Do not create the encounter before the documented time of death.
Required
ii. Patient death attended by RN or Hospice Staff Document who from hospice was present at the time of death or responded to the death call. "This RN responded to death call at [time]. Upon arrival, patient was found unresponsive, no respirations, no pulse. Pronounced at [time] by [RN name / MD name]."
Required
iii. Post-mortem visit note documents: TOD, who pronounced, MD notified, post-mortem cares, funeral home contact and pickup time This note is the complete record of what happened after death — be thorough. Include who was at the bedside, condition of remains, and all notifications made. "Patient expired at 14:22 on [date]. RN [name] pronounced death. Dr. [name] notified at 14:35, order for pronouncement received. Post-mortem cares provided — patient's body bathed, positioned supine. Family (daughter and son-in-law) present and tearful but calm. Funeral home [name] contacted at 14:40, arrived at 16:15. Remains released to funeral home at 16:20."
Required
iv. Medication destruction documented — amount of each narcotic counted and witness named Document every controlled substance: medication name, strength, quantity destroyed, method of destruction, and the witness present. Count narcotics before discontinuing in the Care Plan (click the pill icon). "Morphine 20mg/mL oral solution — 18mL remaining. Destroyed by mixing with coffee grounds and disposing in trash. Witness: [caregiver name], daughter of patient."
Required
v. Family support addressed and documented — coping, grief, bereavement referral noted Describe how the family is responding emotionally, what support was offered at the time of death, and whether a bereavement referral was made. "Daughter and son-in-law present at time of death. Daughter tearful but verbalized she was grateful the patient passed peacefully at home. Family offered bereavement support resources. Social worker notified of patient death for bereavement follow-up."
Required
vi. IDG team notified of patient death and documented Document IDG notification. RN Case Manager creates the IDG custom goal (e.g., "Discharge Planning") for sign-off. IDG resolves the primary care plan problem at the next IDG meeting.
Required
vii. DME pickup scheduled or communicated to DME coordinator If patient had DME (hospital bed, wheelchair, oxygen, etc.), document that pickup was arranged. Mark N/A if patient had no DME.
viii. HOPE discharge assessment completed in FireNote Complete the Discharge HOPE assessment. The back office manages the iQIES submission deadline.
Required

Your documentation must clearly show WHY the prognosis now exceeds six months — what changed, what improved, and what objective findings support this determination. "Patient originally admitted with end-stage COPD, PPS 40%. Over the course of the hospice stay, patient has demonstrated consistent improvement: PPS now 60%, ambulating independently with walker, O2 requirement decreased from 4L continuous to 2L with activity only, weight gain of 8 lbs over 3 months, tolerating full oral diet. Patient reporting significantly improved energy and functional status. IDG consensus: prognosis now exceeds six-month criteria."
Required
v. Communication and coordination with PCP documented Document when the PCP was notified, what was communicated, and who you spoke with. Discharge summary to be faxed upon completion."

Transfer to Another Hospice (Section E) — The patient elects to move to a different hospice provider. Your documentation is the bridge to their new care team. The receiving hospice is taking over the patient's hospice benefit and needs everything: the full medication list, current plan of care, clinical history, and all active orders.
🚫 Do NOT resolve the care plan. Resolving removes the medication list from the discharge summary. The receiving hospice depends on this to continue the patient's care without interruption. Leave the care plan fully active.
🖥️ FireNote: Physician order required. Start encounter before discharge date/time. Do NOT resolve the care plan. Level of Care change completed by Director of Clinical Services. Complete HOPE assessment — back office handles submission.

⚠️
Discharged for Cause (Section F) — Hospice discharges the patient because disruptive, abusive, or uncooperative behavior seriously impairs care delivery or safety. CMS requires that hospice demonstrate genuine effort to resolve the issue before proceeding. This discharge carries the highest documentation burden — every step must be thoroughly documented.
🚨 Document everything. CMS requires evidence that hospice made serious, good-faith efforts to resolve the situation before discharging. Sparse or vague documentation is the most common reason these discharges are challenged. Be specific, objective, and thorough.
🖥️ FireNote: Physician order required. Start encounter before discharge date/time. Level of Care change completed by Director of Clinical Services. Complete HOPE assessment — back office handles submission.

iii. Documentation clearly and objectively supports the basis for discharge for cause Describe the specific behaviors — be factual and objective, not subjective or emotional. Show how the behavior seriously impaired the ability to provide care or created a safety risk for staff. "On [date], caregiver [name] became verbally aggressive toward the RN during a scheduled visit, using threatening language and refusing to allow the nurse to assess the patient. Staff have reported feeling unsafe entering the home on [dates]. Incident reports filed [dates]. The behavior has prevented safe and effective delivery of hospice care on multiple occasions."
Required
iv. Documentation of all efforts made to resolve the situation before initiating discharge List every attempt: conversations, care conferences, plan of care adjustments, social work intervention, etc. CMS requires evidence of genuine effort to resolve — this section must be specific and detailed. "Social worker met with caregiver on [date] to discuss concerns and establish behavioral expectations. Care conference held [date] with patient, caregiver, social worker, and clinical director. Written behavior agreement offered to caregiver on [date] and declined. After continued unsafe incidents, IDG convened [date] and determined discharge for cause is necessary."
Required
v. Documentation that patient/caregiver was informed discharge for cause is being considered The patient must have been given notice that a discharge for cause was being considered — document the date, who was told, and their response. "Patient's daughter (DPOA) notified by clinical director on [date] that hospice is considering discharge for cause due to continued unsafe conduct. Daughter stated she understood the concern. Written notice provided on same date."
Required

Mark N/A if the patient does not have a PCP or this is not applicable to the specific situation. "Dr. [name] notified by phone on [date] of the discharge for cause. Situation summarized.
